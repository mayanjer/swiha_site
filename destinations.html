14 destinations across Uganda, Rwanda, Kenya & Tanzania

Uganda

The Pearl of Africa

Uganda packs extraordinary biodiversity into a small, verdant country — half of the world's mountain gorillas, 1,000+ bird species, and landscapes of remarkable diversity.

Most Popular

Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

Home to half the world's remaining mountain gorillas. This UNESCO World Heritage Site in southwestern Uganda offers the most profound wildlife encounter on earth.

🏔 1,190–2,607m altitude 🦍 400+ gorillas 🌧 Best: June–August

Queen Elizabeth National Park

Uganda's most visited park, famous for its tree-climbing lions, massive hippo pods, Kazinga Channel, and the remarkable Ishasha sector.

🦁 Tree-climbing lions 🐘 5,000+ elephants 🌤 Year-round

Murchison Falls National Park

Watch the mighty Nile force itself through a 7-metre gorge creating the world's most powerful waterfall. Rich in wildlife and spectacular scenery.

💧 World's most powerful falls 🦛 Uganda's largest NP 🌤 Dec–Feb best

Kibale National Park

Home to the highest density of primates in East Africa — 1,500 chimpanzees, 12 other primate species, and over 375 bird species in lush rainforest.

🐒 1,500 chimpanzees 🦜 375+ bird species 🌱 Bigodi Wetland Walk

Jinja — Source of the Nile

East Africa's adventure capital. White-water rafting, kayaking, bungee jumping and quad biking at the source of the world's longest river.

🌊 Grade 5 rapids 🏞 Nile source 🌤 Year-round

Kidepo Valley National Park

Uganda's most remote and arguably most spectacular park. Dramatic semi-arid savannah with lions, cheetah, ostrich and massive buffalo herds.

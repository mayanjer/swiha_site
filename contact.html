Frequently Asked Questions

Gorilla permits sell out quickly, especially for peak season (June–September and December–February). We recommend booking at least 3–6 months in advance. We handle all permit bookings on your behalf.
Cancellations made 60+ days before travel: full refund minus permit costs. 30–59 days: 50% refund. Under 30 days: non-refundable. We strongly recommend travel insurance. Gorilla permits are generally non-refundable as set by UWA.
Yes! We accept a 30% deposit to confirm your booking, with the balance due 30 days before travel. We accept bank transfer, credit/debit card, and mobile money.
We don't arrange visas directly, but we provide detailed guidance on obtaining your Uganda e-visa online. Most nationalities can get a single-entry tourist visa for $50 USD. We also advise on East Africa tourist visas covering Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda.
All our prices include: airport transfers, accommodation (specified in each package), all game park entrance fees, a professional English-speaking guide, fuel and vehicle costs, and meals as specified. Excluded: flights, visas, travel insurance, tips, and personal items.
Yes — Uganda is one of East Africa's safest and friendliest countries for tourists. Our guides and vehicles are fully equipped for safety in the field, and our 12 years of operating with zero serious incidents is testament to our commitment to guest safety.
Absolutely! We welcome solo travellers and can match you with small group departures to keep costs down, or arrange a fully private guided tour. We have extensive experience ensuring solo travellers feel safe, welcomed, and never alone.
Uganda Wildlife Authority requires gorilla trekkers to be at least 15 years old. There is no upper age limit — we have successfully guided guests well into their 70s and 80s on easier gorilla sectors. We'll always match your group to the most appropriate gorilla family and sector based on fitness levels.
